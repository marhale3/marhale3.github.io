A k-input binary truth table is a map from k input bits (binary digits, 0 [false] or 1 [true]) to 1 output bit. For example, the 2-input binary truth tables for the logical AND and XOR functions are:

x y x AND y
000
010
100
111
x y x XOR y
000
011
101
110
